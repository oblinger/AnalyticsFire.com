The Problem: Buying Equation Uncertainty

Being inherently digital, DevOps service engagements produce data about their execution.

But what happens to all this data?

Today that value is being trashed

• Not used to optimize current execution.

• Not used to increase certainty for the next buyer.

The Solution: Evidence-Based IT

The Analytics Fire Platform captures and analyzes data from DevOps service engagements in order to add hard data to the buying equation. Out platform helps buyers and sellers:

  • Discover

    Identify new opportunities to speed development velocity, as well as increase application availability, performance, efficiency, and security.

  • Validate

    Justify specific vendor and technology choices via outcome-driven comparison to decisions made by peers in the same market and technology defined segments.

  • Optimize

    Predict and measure outcomes to optimize business impact.

Our platform uses hard data to reduce uncertainty in the buying equation.

About the Founders

Nick Allen

Having built his professional career leading cross-functional teams to implement complex, mission critical systems in multiple industries, Nick has long wished there was a better way to buy IT-related professional services. Prior to trying to solve this pain with the Analytics Fire Platform, Nick was the Co-founder and VP of Engineering of Ginzametrics, an enterprise-class marketing analytics platform that helps brands drive more traffic and increase revenue from SEO. Nick’s favorite engineering project was in the defence industry, where he led a 30+ person cross-functional team to design and develop a 4g-based tactical communications system for the US Special Forces and paramilitary first responders. Nick has worked in the technology industry in The Netherlands, Poland, India, Israel, and the USA.

Dan Oblinger

He created and lead the DARPA initiative that resulting in the IBM Jeopardy playing system, Watson. He developed the payments platform for PayByGroup a 500-startups funded company where he served at CTO. At IBM Research he implemented a learning by demonstration technology that found it way into the Rational Software tool suite. He taught at Columbia University, and holds a Pd.D. in Computer Science.
